Salmorejo Recipe

Salmorejo is a cold Spanish soup that originates from the Andalusian region. It's a refreshing and velvety blend of tomatoes, bread, olive oil, and garlic . Here's a basic Salmorejo recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg (about 2 pounds) ripe tomatoes, cored and coarsely chopped
  • 200g (about 7 ounces) day-old bread, crusts removed
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled
  • 150ml (about 2/3 cup) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional toppings: hard-boiled eggs, jamón (cured ham) or prosciutto, and a drizzle of olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Soak the Bread: Place the bread in a bowl and add enough water to cover it. Let it soak for about 5-10 minutes, then squeeze out excess water.
  2. Blend Ingredients: In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped tomatoes, soaked bread, peeled garlic, olive oil, and sherry vinegar. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Season: Taste the mixture and season with salt as needed. Adjust the acidity with more sherry vinegar if desired.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate the soup for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the flavors to meld and the soup to chill.
  5. Serve: Ladle the chilled Salmorejo into bowls. Traditionally, it is garnished with hard-boiled eggs (chopped or sliced), jamón or prosciutto, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  6. Optional: Feel free to customize the toppings based on your preferences. Some people like to add diced cucumber or green bell peppers for extra freshness.
  7. Enjoy: Serve the Salmorejo as a refreshing appetizer or light meal, especially during hot summer days.
